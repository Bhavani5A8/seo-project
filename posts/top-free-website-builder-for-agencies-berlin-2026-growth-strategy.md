### Q4: Is Interaction to Next Paint (INP) more important than LCP?

In 2026, INP has become the definitive metric for "User Delight." While LCP measures how fast the page loads, INP measures how responsive the page is when a user actually interacts with it (e.g., clicking a menu or a "Contact Us" button). For a Berlin agency, a high LCP might get a user to your site, but a poor INP will make them leave in frustration. Free builders that use lightweight JavaScript frameworks or "Vanilla JS" for their interactive elements tend to perform better in this regard. You can test your INP using the Chrome DevTools "Performance" tab to ensure that interaction latency stays under 200 milliseconds.

### Q5: How do I implement local German schema for a Berlin-based business?

Local SEO for Berlin requires specific schema types. You should use the `LocalBusiness` or `ProfessionalService` schema, specifically filling out the `address` field with "Berlin, Germany" and the correct `postalCode` (e.g., 10115 for Mitte). Additionally, you should use the `knowsAbout` property to list your agency's core competencies, such as "Brand Design" or "Growth Marketing." Most top-tier free builders allow you to inject this JSON-LD code into the header. By providing this structured data, you help AI search engines understand exactly who you are and which Berlin neighborhoods you serve, increasing your chances of appearing in the "Local Pack."

In 2026, the choice between .de and .com depends on your primary target audience. If 80% of your clients are local German businesses, a .de domain carries a "Trust Signal" that is still highly relevant in the DACH region.
