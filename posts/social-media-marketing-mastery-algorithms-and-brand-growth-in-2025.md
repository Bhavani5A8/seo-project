**Q1: How does social media engagement actually affect my website's Google ranking?**

While social media likes and shares are not "Direct Ranking Factors" in the way backlinks are, they function as powerful "Indirect Signals." **Q2: What is the optimal technical frequency for posting in 2025?**

The optimal frequency is determined by your "Algorithmic Decay Rate"—the time it takes for a post to lose 50% of its reach. On TikTok, this is often 2-3 hours, whereas on LinkedIn, it can be 24-48 hours. To maximize growth without triggering "Spam Filters," you should post at a frequency that ensures you always have one "Active" post in the feed. For most brands, this translates to 1-2 times daily on high-velocity platforms (TikTok/Instagram) and 3-5 times weekly on low-velocity platforms (LinkedIn). Technically, posting more than this can lead to "Internal Competition," where your own posts fight for the same audience's attention, lowering the overall performance of your account.

**Q4: Why do my social videos look blurry after I upload them?**

Blurry video is almost always a result of "Re-Encoding Latency" or "Bitrate Mismatch." If you upload a 4K video at a very high bitrate, the social platform's server has to work extra hard to compress it, often leading to a "Dirty" encode. To fix this technically, export your videos at 1080p with a maximum bitrate of 15 Mbps. Also, ensure you are uploading over a stable 5G or Wi-Fi connection; many apps will lower your upload quality if they detect a weak signal to save bandwidth. This is a client-side technical choice that has a massive impact on the user's perception of your brand's quality.

**Q5: Can I use AI to write my social media captions and still rank well?**

Yes, but with a technical caveat: you must avoid "Algorithmic Homogenization." Algorithms are becoming very good at detecting generic AI patterns. If your captions look like every other AI-generated post, your reach will be throttled. To use AI effectively, provide it with specific "Technical Entities" and "Unique Data Points" to include. Use AI as a structural tool, but manually inject your brand's unique "Voice Vector." From a technical SEO standpoint, ensure your AI-generated captions still follow keyword proximity and density rules to remain discoverable in social search. AI should be your co-pilot, not the sole driver of your content strategy.

**Q6: What is the most important Core Web Vital for social media traffic?**

LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) is the most critical metric for social media traffic. Users coming from social media have an incredibly high "Bounce Propensity." If your page takes more than 1.5 seconds to show its main content, you will lose up to 50% of your visitors before they even see your offer. Social apps have built-in browsers that are often slower than standalone browsers like Chrome or Safari. Therefore, you must optimize your technical stack to be "Extra-Lean." This means minimal JavaScript, optimized images (WebP/AVIF), and a solid Content Delivery Network (CDN) to serve files from the edge nearest the user.
